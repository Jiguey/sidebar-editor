# Startup & Hardware Jokes

*For when the burn rate is high and the coffee is lower.*

---

### 1. The Pitch

**Founder:** "Our product is basically Cursor, but local-first and open."

**VC:** "So it's Cursor but slower, with no funding, and the AI sometimes just describes what it would do instead of doing it?"

**Founder:** "We call that a feature. It builds trust."

---

### 2. Hardware vs Software Debugging

**Software engineer:** "I just fixed a bug in 30 seconds. Pushed to prod."

**Hardware engineer:** "I just spent three weeks tracing a signal that turned out to be a solder bridge thinner than a human hair, invisible to the naked eye, only detectable under a microscope at a specific angle of light, after which I had to reorder the entire PCB batch because the manufacturer already panelized it."

**Software engineer:** "git blame must be brutal for you guys."

---

### 3. Supply Chain Reality

**Startup founder:** "Our supply chain is lean and agile."

**Also startup founder, two weeks later:** "Our PCBs are stuck in Shenzhen customs because of a national holiday I didn't account for, our backup supplier is a guy named Dave who operates out of a unit in an industrial park in Ohio, and the specific capacitor we need has a 38-week lead time. We ship in March."

**Investor:** "Which March?"

---

### 4. The MVP

**What the VC thinks MVP means:** Minimum Viable Product.

**What the engineer thinks MVP means:** The thing we demo that doesn't actually work yet but crashes in a way that looks intentional.

**What the founder thinks MVP means:** Whatever we shipped last Thursday that's already in production and has three open GitHub issues marked "wontfix."

---

### 5. Scaling Ambitions

**CTO in all-hands:** "We need to architect for 10 million concurrent users."

**Actual user count:** 23. One is the founder's brother. Four are bots.

**Backend engineer:** *(quietly commits a Redis config that will never, ever be necessary)*

**CTO:** "That's the startup mindset."

---

### 6. Local AI Is Fine, They Said

**User:** "Why is the AI just describing the tool call instead of running it?"

**Developer:** "The model supports tool calling."

**User:** "Then why—"

**Developer:** "Supports it in the sense that it has read about tool calling. Philosophically. It is aware tool calling exists."

---

### 7. The Hardware Engineer's Nightmare

Why do hardware startups age faster than software ones?

In software, you push a fix and it's live in 30 seconds.

In hardware, you find the bug, redesign the board, wait 3 weeks for fabrication, wait 2 weeks for assembly, wait 1 week in customs, and then discover you fixed the wrong thing.

**Iteration velocity:** one per quarter.
**Gray hairs per iteration:** three.

---

### 8. Funding Math

**Pre-seed pitch:** "We just need $500k to get to product-market fit."

**Six months later:** "We found product-market fit. We need $2M to reach profitability."

**Twelve months later:** "We're profitable on a per-unit basis if you exclude COGS, salaries, AWS, the PCB rework, the lawyer who reviewed our ToS, and the conference we went to in San Francisco where we definitely talked to real potential customers and didn't just eat at good restaurants."

---

*"In a startup, every problem is two weeks away from being solved. It has always been two weeks. It will always be two weeks."* 🚀
